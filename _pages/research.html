  • Reliability (Jul '18 - Present)
    Advisor: Prof. Souvik Mahapatra, Electrical Engineering , IIT Bombay

    • Stress Induced Leakage Current (SILC) and Positive Bias Temperature Instability (PBTI) in High-K Metal Gate (HKMG) MOSFETs

      • Bias Temperature Instability (BTI) remained as the forerunner in the device reliability domain with the continuous transistor scaling; PBTI remains a concern for N-MOSFETs. Bulk trap generation in HK for PBTI stress is estimated from SILC measurements.

      • From SILC measurements of 7 wafers made through different preclean, pre and post HK Ashbury, and different IL and HK thicknesses, extracted out bulk trap densities (NOT) in IL and HK, and used RDD code to model them
      • Using the double interface H/H2 Reaction Diffusion (RD) model, modelling the trap generated from PBTI stress (NIT)

    • Time Dependent Dielectric Breakdown (TDDB) of ultrathin oxides

      • TDDB is a failure mechanism in MOSFETs, when the gate oxide breaks down as a result of long-time application of relatively low electric field.

      • Constructed a cell based MOSFET percolation model which generates bulk and interface traps with varied defect generation and procured the time at which a percolation path emerges using BFS algorithm
      • Contrived the parameters of Weibull distribution from the data obtained and plotted to show that it is indeed characterized by short breakdown times and shallow Weibull slopes

  • Time to Failure Distribution of a Stored Bit in SRAM due to RTN. (Jan '19 - Present)
    Advisor: Prof. Animesh Kumar, Electrical Engineering , IIT Bombay
    Trapped charges in the oxide interface of metal oxide semiconductor field effect transistor (MOSFET) affect the reliability of operation. Trapping and detrapping of charges leads to a random telegraphic noise (RTN) injection and this phenomenon affects the reliability of circuits.

    • Estimated time to failure distribution of a stored bit in an SRAM cell is by the composition of Monte-Carlo simulations and circuit-level abstraction of an SRAM cell
    • Showcased results of proposed estimation procedure for 45nm CMOS technology, with RTN due to a single-trap model, using Cadence Virtuoso
    • Examine effect of process variations, via circuit-level simulations and found the distribution to worsen
